## üèõ The Foundation: 1:1 Net Metering in Florida
The single most important factor for Florida solar ROI is **Net Metering.** Unlike California, which moved to a complex "Net Billing" system (NEM 3.0), Florida still operates under a traditional 1:1 model.

### How it Works
When your solar panels produce more energy than your home needs during the day, the excess flows back onto the grid. Your utility meter literally spins backward. 
- **The Value:** For every kWh you export, you receive a credit worth exactly what the utility would have charged you (e.g., $0.15/kWh). 
- **The "True-Up":** At the end of the year, if you've produced more than you've used, the utility usually pays you at a lower "wholesale" rate, but the goal for most homeowners is to simply zero out their annual consumption bill.

### The Political Landscape
In 2022, Florida's Governor vetoed a bill that would have gutted net metering. While the policy is secure for 2026, the lesson for Florida homeowners is simple: **Lock in your status now.** Systems installed under current rules are typically "grandfathered" into the 1-to-1 rate for 20 years, even if the state changes the law for new customers in the future.

## üí∏ Tax Exemptions: Florida's "Hidden" Solar Discounts
Florida lacks a state income tax, which means the state cannot offer an income tax credit like New York or South Carolina. Instead, Florida provides two powerful "Automatic" tax exemptions that lower your net cost and protect your equity.

### 1. The Sales Tax Exemption
Under Florida Statute 212.08, all solar energy systems and components are **exempt from state sales and use tax.** 
- **The Impact:** On a typical $30,000 solar installation, this saves you **$1,800** immediately at the point of purchase. Your installer should automatically reflect this 0% tax rate in your contract.

### 2. The Property Tax Exclusion
In most home improvements (like adding a pool or a guest house), your property value increases, and your taxes follow suit. Florida law specifically excludes 100% of the value of a renewable energy system from your property tax assessment.
- **The Double Win:** You get the [~4% increase in home resale value](/articles/solar-panels-resale-value) without paying an extra dime in annual taxes.

## üåÄ Hurricane Resilience: The Florida Hybrid System
In 2026, Florida solar is no longer just about the money; it's about the **"Lights Staying On."** Following the devastating hurricane seasons of the mid-2020s, "Grid-Tied with Battery Backup" has become the standard Florida configuration.

### Why Batteries are Different in Florida
In California, batteries are for "Arbitrage" (saving money). In Florida, batteries are for "Resilience" (survival). 
- **The Power Gap:** A standard solar system without a battery **shuts off** during a power outage to prevent back-feeding the grid and injuring line workers. 
- **The Hybrid Fix:** A "Hybrid" system uses an automatic transfer switch (like the ones found in the *Enphase IQ System* or *Tesla Powerwall*) to disconnect from the grid during an outage and create a "Micro-Grid" for your home. You can run your AC, refrigerator, and lights indefinitely as long as the sun continues to shine.

## üìù The Florida Sizing Strategy: ACP vs. Consumption
Because Florida has 1:1 net metering, the sizing strategy is straightforward: **Size for 100% Offset.** 
- **The HVAC Factor:** In Florida, your AC is your biggest expense. We recommend analyzing your July and August bills specifically. If your solar doesn't cover your peak summer usage, you'll still face $200+ bills during the hottest months.
- **The "Solar Ready" Roof:** Given Florida's humidity and salt air, we strongly recommend ensuring your roof has at least 15 years of life remaining before installing. Many Florida homeowners use the **30% Federal Tax Credit** to help offset a combined Roof + Solar project (consult a tax pro for eligibility).
