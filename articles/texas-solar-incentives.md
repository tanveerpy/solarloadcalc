## üèõ The Deregulated Market: Understanding Solar Buyback
The most significant difference between Texas and states like Florida or California is the lack of a state-wide "Net Metering" mandate. In Texas, no one *has* to pay you for your solar energy‚Äîbut many companies *want* to.

### How Solar Buyback Plans Work
In the deregulated parts of Texas (Houston, Dallas-Fort Worth, Corpus Christi, etc.), you choose your electricity provider. These providers offer "Solar Buyback Plans" to attract customers with panels. 
- **1-to-1 Real-Time:** Some plans credit you the full retail rate for every kWh you export, but only in real-time. 
- **Market Price:** Others pay you the "LMP" (Locational Marginal Price), which can be very low at 2 PM but incredibly high during a summer heatwave.
- **The Cap:** Most Texas plans will credit you up to your monthly usage but won't let you "bank" credits for next month or receive a cash check.

## üí∏ Tax Exemptions: The Texas Property Tax Shield
Texas has no state income tax, which means there is no "State Solar Credit." However, Texas makes up for this with one of the most robust property tax exemptions in the United States.

### Section 11.27 of the Texas Tax Code
Under Texas law, the addition of a solar energy system to your property is **100% exempt from property tax assessments.** 
- **The Impact:** Solar panels typically [add about 4% to a home's value](/articles/solar-panels-resale-value). On a $400,000 Texas home, that's a $16,000 increase in value. 
- **The Savings:** Without this exemption, that $16,000 increase would cost you an extra $300 to $500 per year in property taxes (given Texas's high property tax rates). Instead, you get the equity increase for free.

## ‚ö°Ô∏è Municipal Utilities: The "Gold Mine" Cities
If you live in a city with a municipal utility (non-deregulated), your solar ROI is often significantly higher due to direct local incentives.

### Austin Energy (Value of Solar Tariff)
Austin Energy uses a unique "Value of Solar" (VoS) credit. You are billed for everything you use at standard rates, but you are credited for everything you produce at a fixed rate (typically around $0.09 to $0.10 per kWh). This credit is designed to reflect the true environmental and infrastructure value your panels bring to the city.

### CPS Energy (San Antonio Rebates)
San Antonio's CPS Energy offers one of the few remaining direct "Rebate" programs. They provide a flat dollar amount per kW of solar installed (e.g., $2,500 for a standard 10kW system). This is applied on top of the **30% Federal Tax Credit**, significantly lowering the "Net Cost" of your investment.

## ‚ùÑÔ∏è The Resilience Factor: Solar as Grid Insurance
In Texas, the conversation around solar changed forever after the 2021 winter storm. Homeowners realized that being "grid-tied" wasn't enough if the grid itself was down.

### The Rise of the Texas Micro-Grid
In 2026, over 60% of new Texas solar installations include at least one battery (like the *Tesla Powerwall* or *Enphase IQ*). 
- **Self-Sufficiency:** A battery allows you to stay powered through the "ERCOT Scarcity" events when the grid is strained.
- **Cost Avoidance:** During heatwaves, grid prices in Texas can spike to $5.00 per kWh. A solar+battery system allows you to "Opt-Out" of those high prices by running on stored sun.

## üìù Marketing and Sizing in the Lone Star State
Because most Texas buyback plans don't pay you a check for massive surpluses, the sizing strategy is different than in other states.
- **The "80% Strategy":** To maximize ROI, we often recommend sizing for **80% to 90%** of your annual usage. This ensures you aren't "gifting" excess energy to the utility company for free once your bill hits $0.
- **ROI vs. Savings:** In Texas, solar is often a 7-9 year payback. While this is slightly longer than in the past, the "Risk Mitigation" value of having your own power source in a state with a fragile grid is immeasurable.
