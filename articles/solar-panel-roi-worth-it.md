## üíπ The Three Dimensions of Solar Financials
A professional-grade ROI analysis involves more than just "Money In vs. Money Out." To truly understand the value, we must look at three specific metrics used by institutional investors.

### 1. Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
The IRR is the annual rate of growth an investment is expected to generate. 
- **The Solar Edge:** Because solar savings are "Tax-Free," a 15% IRR from solar is equivalent to an 18% or 19% return in the stock market (where you would lose a portion to taxes).
- **The Reality:** In states like Massachusetts, New Jersey, and California, the IRR for a residential solar system often exceeds **20% annually** over 25 years.

### 2. Net Present Value (NPV)
NPV measures how much a future stream of savings is worth in today's money. 
- **The Calculation:** If you invest $15,000 today (net cost) and receive $60,000 in energy savings over 25 years, the NPV calculates the "profit" you've made after accounting for the time value of money.
- **The Result:** For a typical US home, a solar system delivers an NPV of **$25,000 to $45,000**. That is effectively a $40,000 "gift" from your roof.

### 3. Payback Period (The "Break-Even")
This is the most common metric. It is the number of years it takes for your cumulative energy savings to equals your net installation cost.
- **National Median:** 6 to 9 years.
- **The Accelerator:** In 2026, as grid prices spike, we are seeing payback periods in the Northeast and the West Coast drop to **4.5 to 6 years**.

## üöÄ Why Solar ROI is Accelerating in 2026
Two major macroeconomic factors are shrinking the payback period and increasing the IRR for new solar owners today.

### Factor A: Utility Inflation (The Stealth Profit)
Since 2020, residential electricity rates in the U.S. have increased by an average of 4.5% annually. However, in major markets, we've seen single-year spikes of 20% or more. 
Every time your utility raises rates, your solar ROI **increases.** You are essentially "shorting" the utility company; as their price goes up, the value of your self-generated power goes up in direct proportion.

### Factor B: The Capex Compression
While inflation has increased the cost of eggs and lumber, the "Hard Cost" of solar panels and inverters is at an all-time low. Advanced manufacturing in 2026 has made high-efficiency panels (450W+) more affordable than the 300W panels of five years ago. You are getting more "Watts per Dollar" than at any other time in history.
