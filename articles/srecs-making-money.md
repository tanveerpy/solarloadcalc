The Federal Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) helps you *buy* your solar system, but **SRECs (Solar Renewable Energy Credits)** help you *pay* for it over the next decade. While net metering saves you money on your monthly bill, SRECs represent a secondary, liquid asset class that most homeowners don't even know exists. In 2026, SRECs remain one of the most powerful boosters for solar ROI, yet they are often left on the table due to complex registration requirements.

An SREC is a "green attribute" of the energy you produce. By generating clean solar energy, you are doing a service for the environment‚Äîa service that utility companies are legally required to pay for. This guide explains how to "harvest" these credits, how to navigate the brokerage markets, and why an 8kW system in D.C. can generate thousands of dollars in passive income every year.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Key Takeaways**
> - **1 SREC = 1,000 kWh:** For every megawatt-hour your panels produce, you mint one credit that can be sold on the open market.
> - **RPS Requirements:** Utilities buy SRECs to comply with state Renewable Portfolio Standards (RPS). If they don't buy credits from you, they must pay a heavy fine to the state.
> - **Aggregation Necessity:** You cannot sell your SRECs directly to a utility. You must work with an aggregator (broker) who pools thousands of homeowners' credits together for wholesale trading.
> - **The Registration Window:** Most states have a strict 90-day window after your system is turned on to register for SRECs. If you miss this window, you lose the credits for your initial months of production.

## üìú What Exactly is an SREC?
To understand SRECs, you have to separate the "electricity" from the "greenness." When your panels produce a kilowatt-hour of energy:
1. **The Electricity:** You use this in your house or sell it back via [Net Metering](/articles/net-metering-explained).
2. **The SREC:** This is the *proof* that the energy was clean. Utilities need this proof to satisfy state laws. 

Even if you use every drop of solar energy inside your own home, you still "mint" the SREC. You are essentially selling the "bragging rights" of your clean energy to a utility company that is currently burning coal or gas.

## üìà The SREC Lifecycle: How to Get Paid
Turning photons into cash requires a multi-step administrative process.

### 1. Revenue-Grade Monitoring
To participate in an SREC market, your system must have a "Revenue-Grade Meter" (RGM). This is a highly accurate meter (ANSI C12.20 standard) that is typically integrated into high-end [Microinverters](/articles/inverter-types-compared). Standard utility meters are often not accurate enough for SREC minting.

### 2. Certification & GATS/NEPOOL
Once your system is installed, your installer (or you) must register it with the state utility commission and the regional registry (like GATS in the PJM region or NEPOOL in New England). This creates a digital account where your credits are "born."

### 3. Minting & Selling
Every time your inverter reports another 1,000 kWh of lifetime production, 1 SREC is minted in your account. Most homeowners use an **Aggregator** (like *SRECTrade* or *SolSystems*) to sell these. The aggregator takes a small commission (3-5%) and handles the auction process, depositing the cash directly into your bank account.

## üí∞ Spot Markets vs. Fixed Contracts
How you sell your credits determines your risk profile.
- **The Spot Market:** You sell each SREC at the current market price when it is minted. This is great during a "Credit Crunch" when prices spike, but risky if the market becomes oversupplied.
- **Fixed-Price Contracts:** You lock in a set price (e.g., $180/SREC) for 5 or 10 years. This provides the ultimate [financial stability](/articles/solar-panel-roi-worth-it) and is preferred by banks if you are using the SREC income to pay off a solar loan.

## üèõ The "REC-Sharing" Fine Print: A Warning
In the era of "Free Solar" and [Solar Leases](/articles/solar-lease-vs-buy), some companies hide a clause in the contract that says: **"Developer shall retain all Environmental Attributes and Solar Renewable Energy Credits."**
If you sign a lease with this clause, the solar company installs the panels, but *they* keep the thousands of dollars in SREC checks every year. 
**Rule of Thumb:** If you own the system, you must own the SRECs. If your installer says they "don't do SRECs," find a new installer.

## üìù Critical Deadlines and Eligibility
- **The Catch-back:** Some states allow you to "look back" and mint credits for the 3-6 months *before* you registered. Others are "Day-Forward" only. If you wait a year to register, you could lose $2,000 in missed income.
- **Program Duration:** SREC programs usually have an expiration date (typically 10 to 15 years after installation). Your financial projection should account for the SREC income "dropping off" eventually while your electricity savings continue for 25+ years.
