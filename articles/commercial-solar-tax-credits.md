## üèõ The Commercial "Tax Stack": Layering the Incentives
The secret to the 3-year commercial payback is "stacking" multiple independent incentives into a single project.

### 1. Section 48 Investment Tax Credit (ITC)
For a business, the ITC works much like the residential version but is filed under Section 48. It provides a 30% credit for the entire project cost. 
- **Transferability:** A unique feature of the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act is "Transferability." If a company doesn't have enough tax liability to use the credit, they can now **sell** the credit to another taxpayer for cash (typically at ~90 cents on the dollar), providing immediate liquidity for the project.

### 2. MACRS Depreciation (The Secret Save)
Under the **Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS)**, solar energy systems are categorized as 5-year property. 
- **The Value:** A business can deduct the cost of the system from its taxable income over five years. 
- **The Basis Rule:** Per IRS rules, when you take the 30% ITC, you must reduce your "depreciable basis" by half of the credit's value (15%). This means you get to depreciate **85%** of the system's cost.
- **The Result:** For a company in the 21% federal tax bracket, MACRS provides a "Tax Shield" equal to approximately **18%** of the total project cost.

### 3. USDA REAP Grants: The Rural Multiplier
The **Rural Energy for America Program (REAP)** is the most powerful‚Äîand most overlooked‚Äîcommercial incentive. It is available to agricultural producers and small businesses located in rural areas (typically towns with <50,000 residents).
- **The Offering:** Grants of up to **50%** of total project costs.
- **The Math:** 30% ITC + 20% MACRS Value + 50% REAP Grant = **100% of the project is paid for by the government.** In rural America, solar isn't an investment; it's a gift.

## üèó Roof vs. Carport vs. Ground-Mount
Commercial properties have the unique ability to monetize space that is otherwise useless.

1. **Rooftop Ballasted Systems:** For flat commercial roofs, "ballasted" mounts use concrete blocks to hold the panels down rather than drilling holes. This preserves the roof warranty and is the fastest way to deploy solar.
2. **Solar Carports:** This is the #1 "High-Visibility" solar move. It shades employee or customer cars from the sun/rain while generating massive amounts of power. It also serves as a permanent, visible commitment to the company's **ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)** goals.
3. **Utility-Scale Ground Mounts:** Best for manufacturing plants or data centers with adjacent vacant acreage. These allow for "Single-Axis Tracking" systems that follow the sun from east to west, increasing production by 20% over fixed Tilt systems.

## üß™ Technical Metric: LCOE and Peak Shaving
A business should not look at "Utility Bill Savings" in a vacuum. A CFO should target a **Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE)**‚Äîthe total cost of the system divided by the total kWh it will produce over 30 years.
- **Grid Cost:** $0.14 to $0.22 per kWh.
- **Solar LCOE:** $0.05 to $0.08 per kWh.
By "locking in" an LCOE of 7 cents for the next 30 years, the business is effectively buying insurance against future grid inflation.

### The Power of Peak Shaving
Most commercial bills have a "Demand Charge"‚Äîyou are billed based on the highest amount of power you pulled in a single 15-minute window. If your factory turns on all its machines at 10 AM, you pay a massive fee. Pairing solar with a commercial battery (like the *Tesla Megapack*) allows for "Peak Shaving"‚Äîdischarging the battery during those 15 minutes to keep your demand artificially low, resulting in savings that can often exceed the energy generation itself.
